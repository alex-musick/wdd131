About

If you're tired of using online calendars full of confusing features, if you want to keep your digital calendar offline, or if you just want something simpler than everything else, then Dumb Calendar is for you!

Dumb Calendar allows you to simply write a note in whatever format you'd like, and have that note appear on the day that you have associated it with. Store relevant to-do lists, itineraries, reminders, or whatever other text you want.

Make sure to click the "Save Contents" button after you update your calendar entry!

Frequently Asked Questions

Why would I want this?

Most calendar applications are built around tasks or events that fit into specific slots of time. This does not fully reflect everything that somebody might write on a paper calendar. By allowing the user to simply type whatever they want, more flexibility is afforded while also making the software easier to use.

Why did you make this?

I made this as my final project for the class WDD 131: Dynamic Web Fundamentals at BYU-Idaho.

Does Dumb Calendar store my data online?

No. Your data is stored entirely on your local computer.

Can I switch computers or web browsers and keep my calendar?

Maybe. If your new web browser or computer allows you to migrate your web browser data, then your calendar *might* be able to migrate with it. However, there is no way to export your data directly.
